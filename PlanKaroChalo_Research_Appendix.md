## Survey Breakdowns

### Prateek's Cohort Survey (49 respondents, 15 questions)

**Planning effort distribution:** 1-2 people did most of it (55%), one person did everything (12%), fairly shared (25%), everyone contributed (8%).

**Most stressful planning stage:** Aligning dates (~35%), building itinerary (~22%), deciding destination (~15%), making bookings (~15%), during-trip coordination (~8%), settling expenses (~5%).

**Budget discussion:** Rough sense (38%), no discussion (30%), clear upfront (22%), caused friction (4%).

**Stress levels:** Average 2.7/5. Organizers: 3.4. Passive members: 1.8.

### Shreya's Cohort Survey (40 respondents, 16 questions)

**Most time-consuming stage (multi-select):** Coordinating dates (~50%), finding/deciding accommodation (~47%), building itinerary (~32%), deciding destination (~26%), figuring out transport (~18%), managing budget (~10%).

**Why trips didn't happen:** Dates couldn't align — 35% (14 respondents). Momentum died — 20% (8). Last-minute dropouts — 17% (7). All trips happened — 15% (6). Budget — 7% (3). Accommodation — 5% (2).

**Response to non-responders:** Call/personally message (62%), planning slows significantly (15%), decide without them (12%), wait (5%).

### Prateek's Detailed Survey (11 respondents, 25 questions)

**Organizer time:** <2 hours (18%), 2-5 hours (18%), 5-15 hours (27%), 15+ hours (36%).

**Information in WhatsApp:** Pinned but hard to find (27%), separate doc used (45%), organizer saves elsewhere (18%), gets lost (9%).

**Willingness to pay:** Nothing (36%), ₹49-99 (27%), ₹149-299 (18%), ₹500+ (18%).

## Key Quotes by Theme

### Date Alignment
- "Less communication chaos — the communication chaos is because of the dates to align." — Rakshitha
- "Dates. Getting 4 people with different jobs and leave policies to agree on the same 3 days." — Praveen
- "Finalizing the dates and making sure everyone agrees to it" — Shreya survey respondent
- "Getting people to actually commit to dates when you're asking nicely? Forget it." — Arjun (family coordinator)
- "Once dates are locked, everything else feels way easier in comparison." — College student

### Organizer Tax
- "Others expect that the one who initiated the trip will do all the work" — Abhilash Kumar (survey)
- "Commitment issues, last minute backouts, no suggestions in the planning phase — but complaints later." — Aashik Villa (survey)
- "If I didn't do it, it was going to happen. So yeah." — College student

### No Single Source of Truth
- "A dedicated platform where the itinerary lives, everyone can see it, bookings are attached." — Shreya
- "Things which are important get lost in WhatsApp group chats and creates the need to scroll through and find things." — Shreya survey respondent
- "One place where everything lives and people could actually see the plan without me having to repeat it seventeen times." — Arjun

### Passive Participation
- "Give me concrete options with information, not open-ended questions. A poll with context." — Rohith
- "People don't respond properly. No follow-ups. Dates not decided. People go silent." — Aditya Deepak

### Momentum Loss
- "Too much noise on the WhatsApp group. People don't respond properly. No follow-ups. Dates not decided. People go silent. Disagreements, friction. This is where most trips fail." — Aditya Deepak
- "Keeping the momentum up so that plans don't die eventually" — Shreya survey respondent

### Budget
- "Nobody wants to open Splitwise when they're in vacation mode." — Amit
- "Social guilt about budget or destination, domestic vs international" — Rakshitha (survey)
- "Budget alignment before the trip and one person should be the sole responsible for payments during the trip." — Vani (survey)

### What Users Want
- "Don't call it a planner. That already sounds like work. Make it feel like: decide your trip in 60 seconds." — College student
- "I don't want something that makes planning better. I want something that makes planning shorter." — College student
- Wished for "curated, group-profile-specific itinerary suggestions" — Piyush
